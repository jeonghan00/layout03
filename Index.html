HolidayNew Year- New Year is the celebration of the beginning of a new calendar year. It is a time for people to reflect on the past, make resolutions for the future, and celebrate with fireworks, parties, and gatherings with family and friends. — January 1, 1996
HolidayEDSA People Power Anniversary - The EDSA People Power Anniversary commemorates the peaceful revolution of February 1986 in the Philippines, when millions of Filipinos gathered at EDSA (Epifanio de los Santos Avenue) to protest against dictatorship. Through unity, courage, and nonviolent action, the movement restored democracy and freedom in the country. — February 25, 1996
HolidayMaundy Thursday - Maundy Thursday is part of Holy Week in the Christian tradition. It commemorates the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with His disciples before His betrayal and crucifixion. On this day, many Christians attend church services, reflect on Jesus’ sacrifice, and participate in rituals such as the washing of the feet, which symbolizes humility and service. — April 4, 1996
HolidayGood Friday - Good Friday is one of the most solemn days in the Christian calendar. It commemorates the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ at Calvary. On this day, many Christians observe fasting, prayer, and church services to reflect on Christ’s suffering and sacrifice for humanity’s salvation. — April 5, 1996
HolidayAraw ng Kagitingan - Araw ng Kagitingan, or Day of Valor, honors the bravery of Filipino and American soldiers who defended Bataan during World War II. It commemorates the Fall of Bataan in 1942 and the sacrifices made by those who endured the Bataan Death March. The day serves as a reminder of courage, resilience, and patriotism. — April 9, 1996
HolidayLabor Day - Labor Day is dedicated to honoring the contributions and hard work of workers across different fields. It recognizes their role in building society and promoting progress. In the Philippines, it is also a day when labor groups hold activities, rallies, and celebrations to advocate for workers’ rights and better working conditions. — May 1, 1996
HolidayIndependence Day - Independence Day marks the proclamation of Philippine independence from Spanish rule on June 12, 1898, in Kawit, Cavite. It is a celebration of freedom, sovereignty, and national pride. On this day, Filipinos honor the heroes who fought for liberty and reflect on the nation’s journey toward self-determination. — June 12, 1996
HolidayNational Heroes Day - National Heroes Day honors the bravery and sacrifice of all Filipino heroes, both known and unknown, who fought for the nation’s freedom and dignity. It is observed every last Monday of August and serves as a reminder of patriotism, unity, and the enduring spirit of the Filipino people. — August 26, 1996
HolidayAll Saints' Day - All Saints’ Day is a Christian tradition that honors all the saints and departed faithful. In the Philippines, families visit cemeteries, light candles, offer flowers, and pray for their loved ones who have passed away. Even though it wasn’t legally declared as a nationwide holiday in 1996, it was widely practiced and respected as a day of remembrance. — November 1, 1996
HolidayBonifacio Day - Bonifacio Day commemorates the birth anniversary of Andres Bonifacio (1863–1897), known as the Father of the Philippine Revolution. He was the founder of the Katipunan, the secret society that launched the fight against Spanish colonization. This day honors his courage, leadership, and his role in igniting the Filipinos’ struggle for independence. — November 30, 1996
HolidayChristmas Day - Christmas is more than just a holiday—it is a season of light and hope. It brings families together, fills hearts with joy, and reminds us of the gift of love shown through the birth of Jesus Christ. It is a time when kindness shines brighter than the lights on a Christmas tree, and giving becomes the true spirit of celebration. — December 25, 1996
HolidayRizal Day - Rizal Day commemorates the death anniversary of Dr. José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, who was executed on December 30, 1896, in Bagumbayan (now Luneta, Manila). The day honors his patriotism, writings, and sacrifice, which inspired the Filipino people to fight for freedom from Spanish rule. — December 30, 1996
HolidayLast Day of the Year - The Last Day of the Year is a special non-working holiday in the Philippines. It is a time for families and friends to prepare for New Year’s Eve celebrations, marked by gatherings, food, and traditions such as fireworks and “media noche” (midnight feast). It symbolizes saying goodbye to the past year and welcoming the opportunities of the year to come. — December 31, 1996
